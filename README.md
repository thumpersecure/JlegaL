## What This Site Is

**[jlegal.pro](https://jlegal.pro)** is a consolidated legal documentation site for the case of **Justin Horn v. Greystar / Goldtex Apartments** (315 N. 12th St, Philadelphia, PA).

It documents a sequence of events in which a property management company's response to a broken HVAC system — a portable air conditioner sealed with thermally degrading tape — resulted in toxic chemical off-gassing into a tenant's home, a documented medical emergency, and a recorded coercive ultimatum delivered by a property manager who had already been told, on video, that the tenant was sick.

> **The central criminal statute is 18 Pa.C.S. §2705 — Recklessly Endangering Another Person (M2).**
> The site documents not just the harm, but the moment knowledge was established: a recorded video in the Goldtex leasing office, April 15, 2026.

This is a living evidence repository. Every page is citation-grounded and cross-referenced.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The video proves she knew. The FLIR, IoT, and medical records prove what she knew about.**
> These are not parallel evidence tracks — they are cause and effect. Together, they complete the charge.

## The Case in Plain Language

### 1. The HVAC Failed — and Was Never Repaired

In **September 2025**, the central HVAC system serving Unit 806 at Goldtex Apartments broke down. Greystar's solution was not to repair it. Instead, management installed a **portable air conditioning unit** as a stopgap.

### 2. The Stopgap Became the Source

The portable unit's exhaust hose was sealed with **FSK (foil-scrim-kraft) tape** — a metallic adhesive product not rated for sustained contact with hot exhaust air. As the tape thermally degraded, it off-gassed **volatile organic compounds (VOCs)** into the unit's living space, including:

| Compound | Classification |
|---|---|
| Toluene | Neurotoxin, CNS depressant |
| Xylene | Neurotoxin, respiratory irritant |
| Styrene | Possible carcinogen (IARC Group 2A) |
| Formaldehyde | Known carcinogen (IARC Group 1) |

### 3. The Evidence Was Assembled

Justin identified the degradation source using **FLIR thermal imaging**, which showed surface temperatures of **102–113°F** on the tape and exhaust assembly — well above safe operating range for FSK adhesive. This was corroborated by:

- **IoT sensor data** — 3,460 readings from Unit 806 documenting air quality and temperature anomalies
- **Dr. Mark Fabi's physician letter** (April 10, 2026) — documenting chemical exposure-consistent symptoms
- **SERVPRO refusal to remediate** — the contractor stated the contamination exceeded residential scope

### 4. The Video: Mens Rea Established on April 15, 2026

On **April 15, 2026**, Justin went to the Goldtex leasing office. The encounter was **recorded on video**. He told property manager **Nicole Cordial** directly:

> *"I need it. I can't breathe with the air that's come in there. I got sick. I have doctor's notes and you guys know that."*

She did not dispute it. She did not offer a unit transfer. She did not offer HVAC repair. She did not offer equipment evaluation or an industrial hygiene assessment.

**She offered three options:**

1. Reconnect the portable AC — the same unit that had been making him sick
2. Lose the AC entirely — leaving an 88°F unit with no cooling in summer
3. Break the lease and leave

**This is the mens rea anchor.** After this moment, Greystar had documented, first-person, video-recorded knowledge that the unit was causing illness — and took no action to remediate.

### 5. The Medical Emergency: May 6, 2026

**19 days after the leasing office meeting**, Justin was transported by ambulance to an emergency room, where he received oxygen and IV fluids.

## Legal Framework

<details>
<summary><strong>Controlling Statutes</strong></summary>

**18 Pa.C.S. §2705 — Recklessly Endangering Another Person (REAP)**
> *"A person commits a misdemeanor of the second degree if he recklessly engages in conduct which places or may place another person in danger of death or serious bodily injury."*

The video of April 15, 2026 establishes that Nicole Cordial had actual, first-person, contemporaneous notice that the portable AC unit was causing illness — and elected not to remediate. This satisfies the *recklessness* element: conscious disregard of a known risk.

**18 Pa.C.S. §2702(a)(1) — Aggravated Assault**
Potential elevated charge if the medical evidence supports that serious bodily injury was caused or that the conduct was intentional in the legal sense.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Causal vs. Parallel Framing</strong></summary>

The evidence in this case runs **causally**, not in parallel:

- **The video** = mens rea. It proves she *knew*.
- **The FLIR data, IoT readings, and medical records** = actus reus. They prove *what she knew about*.

These are not two separate tracks of evidence. They are cause and effect. The video is the moment knowledge was established. Everything before it is what she was told about. Everything after it is what she chose not to stop.

</details>
