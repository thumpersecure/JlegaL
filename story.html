The Story

Justin Horn  |  Philadelphia  |  2025–2026

A Jewish tenant was assaulted, poisoned, and displaced. Three institutions that should have protected him instead converged to erase him.

The ambulance pulled up to Goldtex Apartments on May 6, 2026. Justin Horn was on oxygen by the time they got him inside. Dizziness, cognitive impairment, confusion—the same cluster that had hit before, but worse this time. The portable AC in his unit had been running that morning, blowing air through ductwork sealed with foil tape that had been off-gassing volatile compounds for a month. Two EMS personnel would later report acute dizziness in the hallway themselves.

His landlord knew. His doctor had written a letter. The property manager had been told on camera and didn’t deny it. She offered three options—all of which continued the harm. None of them was “we’ll fix it.” The building’s rental license had already been expired for two months. The central HVAC had been down since September. SERVPRO would later refuse the remediation job. Exceeds residential scope.

But the ambulance wasn’t the beginning. It was the place where three separate stories met.

This is how the story ends. But it started in three different places.
